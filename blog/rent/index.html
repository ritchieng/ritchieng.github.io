RENT: Reinforcement Learning via Entropy Minimization

A Fully Unsupervised Method to Enhance Model Confidence and Reasoning

Executive Summary

Traditional reinforcement learning often depends on task-specific rewards. RENT provides rewards solely based on a model's confidence, quantified via entropy.

Conceptual Overview

  • No External Labels: Fully unsupervised.
  • Entropy as Confidence: Lower entropy = Higher confidence.
  • Dense Signal: Available reward at every step.
  • Normalized Reward: Entropy averaged over sequence length.

Technical Explanation & Numerical Example

1. Token-Level Entropy

Entropy at step $t$:

\[ \mathcal{H}(p_t) = -\sum_{v \in \mathcal{V}} p_t(v) \log p_t(v) \]

2. RENT Reward

Negative average entropy over $T$ steps:

\[ R(\mathbf{y}_{\text{pred}}) = -\frac{1}{T} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \mathcal{H}(p_t) \]
